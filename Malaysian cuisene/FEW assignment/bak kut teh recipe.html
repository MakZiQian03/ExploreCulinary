Bak Kut Teh Recipe

Ingredients:

- 500g of pork ribs, cut into pieces

- 6 cloves of garlic, crushed

- 4 shallots, sliced

- 1 packet of Bak Kut Teh herbs

- 2-3 pieces of angelica root (optional)

- 2-3 pieces of codonopsis root (optional)

- 2 tablespoons of soy sauce

- 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce

- 1 teaspoon of salt (adjust to taste)

- 1/2 teaspoon of white pepper (or to taste)

- 1.5 liters of water

- 1 tablespoon of goji berries (optional)

- 100g of mushrooms (optional)

- Fried garlic (for garnish)

- Coriander (for garnish)

Instructions:

1. Clean and rinse the pork ribs thoroughly. Soak the Chinese herbs in water for 15 minutes if using.

2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork ribs and blanch for 5 minutes to remove impurities. Drain and rinse the ribs.

3. In the same pot, add 1.5 liters of fresh water, cleaned pork ribs, crushed garlic, sliced shallots, and Bak Kut Teh herbs. Add soaked Chinese herbs and goji berries if using.

4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until ribs are tender. Skim off impurities as needed.

5. Add soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, and white pepper to the broth. Adjust seasoning to taste.

6. Add mushrooms in the last 30 minutes of cooking if using.

7. Serve hot, garnished with fried garlic and fresh coriander. Accompany with steamed rice and preserved vegetables if desired.
